Myth Busters

Botox Myths Busted: What’s True and What’s Not

  • Myth: Botox will freeze your face.
    Reality: In correct doses it relaxes targeted muscles to soften lines — not freeze your expression.
  • Myth: Botox is dangerous.
    Reality: It’s a prescription medicine used safely for decades in trained hands.
  • Myth: Botox is only for women.
    Reality: Men are an increasing group of users — many want a refreshed look for work or confidence.
  • Myth: Botox is permanent.
    Reality: Effects typically last 3–4 months and then wear off gradually.

As a healthcare professional and prescriber, I ensure treatments are discreet, tailored, subtle, and safe.

Who is Suitable for Botox?

Ideal for softening dynamic lines such as forehead lines, frown lines and crow’s feet.

  • Typically suited for ages 25–65 seeking subtle, natural enhancement.
  • Not suitable during pregnancy/breastfeeding, certain neurological conditions, or allergies to components.

A thorough medical screening and consultation are always completed before treatment.

Wrinkle-Relaxing vs Dermal Fillers: What’s the Difference?

Wrinkle-relaxing (Botox): reduces movement lines by relaxing specific muscles.

Dermal fillers: restore volume and structure where collagen has reduced (e.g., lips, cheeks, nasolabial folds).

I’ll advise the best option(s) for your goals at consultation.

Why Choose a Medically-Led Provider?

  • Full medical assessment and prescribing by a qualified practitioner.
  • Medicines sourced from regulated UK pharmacies.
  • Complication recognition and management protocols in place.

With extensive NHS experience, I prioritise safety and transparency. Always check your provider’s credentials.

Your Botox Aftercare — Do’s & Don’ts

Do: gentle facial movements, stay upright 4 hours, follow personalised advice.

Don’t: rub the area for 24 hours; avoid strenuous exercise, saunas and alcohol on the day.

Full effect is seen by 10–14 days. A review at 2–3 weeks is included.
